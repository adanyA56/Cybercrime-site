What is Cybercrime?

Cybercrime is any criminal activity that involves a computer, networked device, or a network. While most cybercrime is carried out to generate profit for the cybercriminals, some cybercrime is carried out against computers or devices directly to damage or disable them. Cybercrime can range from identity theft and hacking to distributing child pornography and other serious offenses.

Types of Cybercrime

  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information.
  • Hacking: Unauthorized access to data in a system or computer.
  • Identity Theft: Stealing personal information to commit fraud.
  • Cyberbullying: Harassing individuals through digital platforms.
  • Ransomware: Holding data hostage until a ransom is paid.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication.
  • Regularly update your software and antivirus programs.
  • Be cautious of suspicious emails and links.
  • Educate yourself about the latest threats.

Report Cybercrime

If you believe you are a victim of cybercrime, report it immediately to your local authorities or online at:

  • FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)
  • Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
